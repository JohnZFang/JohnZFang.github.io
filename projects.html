ECE 686 | Filtering and Control of Stochastic Linear Systems
This project requires us to reproduce/refine a paper. I'd like to find a paper that intersects with the lecture and my research, which makes it not only a project but inspires me somehow. This paper considers a multi-objective filter design for stochastic system which exists the data packet drops possibility network, which gives a compromise between robustness and transient performance.

The game theory in this paper looks interesting while I’m also handling the trade-off between $\mathcal{H}_2$ and $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norms in my research. The path to attain Nash equilibrium takes some effort to go, but it’s a good recall of the details in the proof of Kalman filter in the lectures. Finally, two cross-coupled modified AREs are given and I benchmarked the algorithm by following the same numerical example in the paper. ECE 700 | Distributed Optimization
Novel distributed optimization concepts and algorithms are introduced in this course. Fixed Point Iteration (FPI) is the skeleton of the course, every algorithm developed will be combined with FPI to address optimization problems. And even better, their convergences are guaranteed after proving the FPI convergence theorem.

A partially distributed algorithm is implemented to a wireless sensor network for detecting the wildfires images. We adapt the logistic regression to train our classifier, and solve this problem using gradient descent with a centralized coordinator. The idea of the algorithm is:

  1. Each sensor computes $\nabla f_i (x^k)$ and sends it back to the central coordinator
  2. The central coordinator gathers $\nabla f_i (x^k)$ from all of the sensors, performs the update $$x^{k+1} =x^k −\alpha \nabla f(x^k),$$ and broadcasts $x^{k+1}$ to all the sensors
I tested different $\alpha$ and added the term $\frac{\rho}{2}\omega^\intercal \omega$ to $f (x)$, after choosing smallish $\rho$ (trade-off idea was recalled again), which revealed the strengths of the augmented terms by shortening convergence time dramatically [code].

Bachelor Thesis

Algebraic Connectivity Analysis of Directed Double-Ring Network
Algebraic connectivity is a metric to network synchronization. This project explores the characteristic polynomial of the Laplacian matrix of the network composed by directed double rings by flow graph theory, and executed the inverse power method with displacement to illustrate the relationship of algebraic connectivity $\lambda_2$ and properties of the network.

Mathematical Modeling

China Undergraduate Mathematical Contest in Modeling (CUMCM)
A chemical industrial problem about preparation of C4 olefins by ethanol coupling is considered with respect to the laboratory data. We seek nonlinear ODEs to clarify the interplay of intermediates and analyze the existing oscillations in the data. Furthermore, we verify the catalyst performance by the Lyapunov asymptotically stability. More details can refer to [poster].
